About

Multimodal agents, personalization, and trustworthy LLM behavior

I am currently pursuing the Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence (MSAI) at Nanyang Technological University. My recent work centers on contextual multimodal agents, agent personalization, and memory-grounded AI systems, especially in settings where models need to search, perceive, and reason over long-horizon user data.

Before NTU, I received my B.Sc. in Computer Science from The Chinese University of Hong Kong with First Class Honor. I graduated with a 3.802 / 4.000 major GPA and previously worked at ARISE Lab on LLM memorization, data privacy, and tokenization-related security risks.

Research

Current directions

Contextual Multimodal Agents

Building and evaluating agents that search, perceive, and reason over long-horizon, heterogeneous digital environments with grounded multimodal evidence.

Personal AI and Memory Systems

Grounding personalization in file-system-scale behavioral traces, user context, and memory architectures that stay faithful to multimodal evidence.

LLM Memorization, Privacy, and Security

Studying how data properties and tokenization shape memorization behavior, leakage risks, and privacy vulnerabilities in large language models.
